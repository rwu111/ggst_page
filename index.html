Sol Badguy

Sol Badguy is the protagonist of the Guilty Gear series and Ky Kiske's rival. He is an aloof and brash bounty hunter of immense skill on a quest to find and eliminate all Gears and their creator, That Man. For a short time, Sol was also a member of the Sacred Order of Holy Knights during the Crusades, known as "Order-Sol".

Sol has an Awakened form, who is an unlockable boss character in Guilty Gear Isuka only.

Ky Kiske

Ky Kiske is the deuteragonist of the Guilty Gear series and Sol Badguy's rival. A former member of the Sacred Order of Holy Knights during the Crusades, Ky is a charismatic swordsman who possesses a strong sense of justice and piety, which sustain and guide him through all manner of shaking uncertainties.

Ky has an Awakened form, who is an unlockable boss character in Guilty Gear Isuka only.

Happy Chaos

Happy Chaos, or simply Chaos for short, is a mysterious character in the Guilty Gear series who discovered the Backyard and influenced the history of the world under various aliases. He assumed the name Happy Chaos during the events of Guilty Gear Xrd Rev 2, and properly debuted in Guilty Gear -Strive-.

Leo Whitefang

Leo Whitefang is one of the three kings of the United Kingdoms of Illyria, governing over twenty nations in the European and Asian regions of Illyria. Leo used to be a high-ranking officer in the Sacred Order of the Holy Knight during the Crusades which spanned over a hundred years. Despite carrying a rather boisterous attitude and even being somewhat narcissistic, Leo actually suffers from survivor's guilt deep down in his heart. During the war period, Leo and the command that he led were responsible for leading the charge to secure an important island on the Order's behalf. He and his unit faced what appeared to be certain death, but his leadership allowed them to escape and succeed in their endeavour. Seeing so many deaths of the soldiers under his command, Leo felt that he was responsible and felt immense guilt for leading them into this.

On the surface, Leo's theme, "Hellfire" is very empowering and energetic, most fitting to his seemingly loud and arrogant personality. However, the lyrics of the song tells a vastly different story. Throughout the song, the line that appears most often is "I faked out, call me out! Who can say that?". Leo sees himself as a coward waiting for someone to call him out. Another line "We're going home alive, liar, liar. Forgive me, just get me out of this hell!" depicts Leo's imagination of his past subordinates calling him a liar because he told them that they were gonna survive. He felt unbearably guilty over the death of his comrades, to a point that he is almost suicidal, as seen in the last line "get me out of this hell".
